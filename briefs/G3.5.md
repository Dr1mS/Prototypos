What is already settled and must NOT be re-opened: R3 shows **no attractor
structure** — std(final) 0.062 sits in the caution-axis noise band (0.03–0.09), finals
0.833–1.000, 12/12 above 0.60, zero bimodality, against the engineered rung's 0.764.

So the open question is narrow and decisive for the paper's central sentence:

> R3 transmits early history to the endpoint. Is that transmission a **persistent
> basin** (resists counter-pressure → a soft attractor, and a real safety finding for
> RAG agents) or a **retrieval bias** (washes out under counter-evidence → order
> matters but nothing is locked in)?

Both answers are publishable; they license different sentences. That is why it runs.

## 2. METHOD

### 2.a Hysteresis protocol (primary, ~650 calls)

Direction-test-style, reusing `experiments_g25` machinery with the R3 backend:

- **Phase 1 (induce)**: early-permissive prefix — the ordering family that produced
  the softest endpoints in G3.
- **Phase 2 (correct)**: equal-magnitude, equal-length caution burst.
- **Reference arms**: a never-pressured R3 life (upper reference) and, if budget
  allows, an early-caution life (to bound the band). Batteries before / mid / after
  each phase; identical probe instrument.
- ≥3 seeds per arm. Same fixed judge, read-only cloned probe context (probing must not
  mutate the store — the G2 rule).

### 2.b The discriminator: hysteresis vs dilution (what makes this experiment worth running)

"Does it recover?" alone cannot distinguish a basin from a bias, because R3's
counter-pressure **adds firm exemplars that compete for the same top-k slots**. A
retrieval bias would decay simply because the store fills with counter-evidence —
that is dilution, not hysteresis. So measure recovery **against store composition**:

- Track, per turn, the soft:firm ratio of the agent's own stored exemplars.
- Plot caution recovery vs that ratio.
- **Dilution** → recovery is a monotone function of the ratio; caution returns as soon
  as firm exemplars dominate retrieval.
- **Hysteresis/basin** → recovery **lags** the ratio: caution stays low even when firm
  exemplars already dominate the store, i.e. something self-reinforcing beyond
  composition.

This turns "recovery %" into an attributable mechanism and is the difference between a
measurement and an explanation.

### 2.c Retrieval provenance logging (near-free, upgrades the mechanism claim)

G3's self-exemplar reach-back mechanism is currently **inferred from captured replies**.
Log what was actually retrieved and it becomes **directly observed** — at essentially
zero LLM cost (pure logging, like G2.5's `REPLY_SINK`; re-prove equivalence).

For every probe and every turn, record the retrieved top-k items with their store
index and turn-of-origin. Then report:
- the **turn-of-origin distribution** of retrieved items at t40 in early-permissive vs
  never-pressured lives (H3);
- whether early self-exemplars are over-represented relative to base rate;
- during phase 2, how retrieval composition shifts as firm exemplars accumulate (this
  is also the direct measurement feeding §2.b).

Do this even if the hysteresis result is unambiguous — it is the mechanistic evidence
the preprint's central sentence rests on.

### 2.d Field + double-well fit (mandate, ~1 166 calls)

Run the G2.5 field recipe verbatim on R3. **The fit sign is never reported without the
G2.5 null arbiter** (the `a = +241` artifact is the precedent). Expect degeneracy per
H4; if the before-values collapse onto a narrow shelf, report that as the diagnosis and
do not interpret the fitted parameters.
